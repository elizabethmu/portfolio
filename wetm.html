WeTM


ATM for people who can’t afford access to a bank

Summary

According to the FDIC, 7% of U.S. households are “unbanked”. The unbanked rate of US Latinos is 2x higher than the overall population. WeTM provides an easy way for people who are experiencing economic hardship to deposit and withdraw cash within short distances.

This is my story of creating WeTM — an ATM for everyone — under 48 hours.

Platform

Android

Duration

48 Hours

When

2018

Problem

According to the FDIC, 9 millions families don’t have access to banks. Some banks charge a maintenance fee if your balance is under a certain threshold. For example, an average farm worker earning $440 a week could be subjected to $87 in fees. This means people whose fund is low are actually being punished for opening a savings account. Moreover, when these folks try to send money to their families overseas, say they are wiring $100, the fees they have to pay is a sizable percentage of the money they are wiring.

Summary:
1. Banking rate of Latinas is low
2. It’s not free to open a banking account if your fund is low. You have to pay fees.
3. It’s expensive to wire a small amount of money.

Solution

Our mission is to let users deposit and withdraw money from “remote banks” with zero fees. These “remote banks” are users who have good credibility that can be trusted to hold money. Credibility is calculated by users’s ratings, etc.

My Role

I was the sole designer for this hackathon project. I worked side by side with a product manager and two developers. My responsibilities included interaction design and visual design.

Design Principles

The design principles I followed are from Nielsen Norman’s heuristics:

Consistency
System Feedback
Consistency Use the same icons and words so that our users don't get confused.
System Feedback Provide reassurance when users complete an action. For example, when users place an order for 20 sandwiches, the application will display 20 as a confirmation.

Market Research

Due to time constraints, I used Uber as my main source of design inspirations because how WeTM help users find each other is similar to how Uber helps user find a driver.

Whom did I research: Uber
Why I chose it: It connects users with someone (driver) within close vincinity of their location
What I learned: Make it easy for users to navigate through each function. Provide a clean interface so that users can focus on the task at hand.

Bringing WeTM to life

Considering that most of my users are millennials, I wanted to ensure Dinersaur caters to this demographic. After I finalized the wireframes and interaction design, it was finally time to bring Dinersaur to life.

Product Name

The name WeTM was a wordplay for the word ATM. It means an ATM for everyone and its mission is to help people who come from a disadvantaged economic background and do not have access to a bank account.

Colors

I used blue as the main color. WeTM provides services in the personal finance arena and according to the psychology of colors, blue speaks credibility for the brand.

Typography

I chose Roboto as the font since this is an Android app.

Retrospective

I thrive in a challenging environment

I am personally happy with the app. Other hackathon participants called me out to compliment on the overall look and feel of WeTM. This was the first Android app I had designed. Researching design guidelines, scribbling user flows on paper, validating ideas with the team, transferring pencil wireframes to Sketch, and building an interactive prototype under 48 hours was no small challenge. Building WeTM gave me a great sense of accomplishment.

Teamwork is key

Our product and dev heroes pulled an all-nighter to thoroughly analyze the banking situation and get the code together. There were a couple hiccups during the hackathon, but we quickly redistributed the tasks and successfully presented our product on time.

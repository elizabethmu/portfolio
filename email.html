Order Email


Receive an order confirmation on your phone

Summary

Order Confirmation is an email template sent to users to confirm that their lunch order has been received.

For confidentiality purposes, I have omitted and obfuscated confidential information in this case study. These designs are a reinterpretation of the original.

Platform

Mobile

Duration

2 Days

When

2018

Goal

The goal of this project was to market School Catering and attract potential buyers. My company was participating at a trade show and wanted to show attendees how they could place an order on the spot, check the status on their phone, and grab their meal at our booth.

My Role

I was the sole product designer on an agile team of 2 developers and 2 customer support specialists. My responsibilities included user research, market research, UI, and preference tests.

Design Principles

Since the focus for this project was around visual and content placement, the most important principle I followed was:


Consistency Use the same colors, icons, and typography from School Catering to strengthen brand recognition.

Users

Users are decision makers that purchase products. Based on feedback from colleagues who had attended trade shows in the past, they were almost exclusively school principals.

Market Research

Since this order confirmation is sent to individuals, I looked up examples for B2C with a direct e-Commerce process.


Why I chose them:

Strong Brands They are big names in restaurant, e-commerce, and food delivery
Email They send email confirmations all the time

What I learned from them:

Hierarchy Clear hierarchy of items, quantity, prices and confirmation #
Readability Easy to read content placement

Preference Tests

The goal of this project was to market School Catering and attract potential buyers. My company was participating at a trade show and wanted to show attendees how they could place an order on the spot, check the status on their phone, and grab their meal at our booth.

Things that I iterated on:
Dark Overlay vs. Light Overlay
Dark footer vs. Light footer
Place food image on the left vs. on the right
Notes under each item vs. at the bottom

Dark Overlay vs. Light Overlay

I iterated on hero section and ran a preference test on internal folks. The entire tech team voted for image overlay, however, a conversation with customer support team helped me recall that our users’ top frustration is aging vision and their top priority is readability. Our cutomer support specialitists range between the age of 50 to 60 and they relate better with our users. I decided against image overlay since a dark background was hard to read for our user demographics.

Notes Under Each Item vs. Notes At The Bottom

Another thing that I iterated on was notes placement. Everyone at the company voted for notes under each item because it was easier to read, however, we ended up implementing notes at the bottom because it was faster to implement given we only had two days for this project.

Visual Design

Considering that most of our users are above the age of 60, I wanted to ensure Email Confirmation caters to this demographic. Their biggest need was to make everything easy to see. This means big fonts, big icons, and a clean color scheme that resonates with fresh food.

Colors

I used green in this email template to reinforce brand recognition. Since this email template was a marketing piece and didn’t need to meet the the most strict accessibility requirements (AAA), I just made sure it were AA compliant.

Iconography

I added two icons in this email template to sprinkle some variety to the text-heavy footer.

Both icons were from the Noun Project and I made small tweaks.

Typography

I used Helvetica Neue because it has a wide variety of font weights and styles. The first I did was to font size to at least 18px. Our users often had to zoom in the page to 150% to be able to see the content and bigger font was their top priority. I used Helvetica Neue for two reasons.

  1. It has a variety of font weights and styles to establish hierarchy.
  2. It is a standard sans-serif font that is used by many companies. In other words, you can’t go wrong with this font when there was little time to experiment font pairing.

Design Handoff


From design to deployment

I used Zeplin to hand off design to engineers after the mockup was ready.

Retrospective

Since this was an ad-hoc project announced a weekend before it was due, conceptualizing a “complete experience” had to wait. I think that if we had time to run some testings among actual users, we would have arrived at an even better solution. I also think that if our engineers had resources to modify legacy code, we could place notes under each food item and that would be mistake-proof to know exactly what customization was needed for what food. This was one improvement I called out and my team agreed on but we couldn’t implement due to legacy code.

Impact

I was personally happy with the results. The business certainly was; our email confirmation generated a lot of sales leads at the trade show.

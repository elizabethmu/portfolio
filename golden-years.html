Golden Years


Everything about personal finances

Summary

Wouldn’t it be great if there’s an assistant that takes care of everything money so that you can focus on living your life? Golden Years manages and presents personal finance in a way that is easy to understand. Whether it’s 401k, investments, or credit card purchases, Golden Years got your back.

This is my story of creating Golden Years — an iOS app that takes care of everything money so people can live the life they want.

Platform

iOS

Duration

8 weeks

When

2018

Problem

Finance apps usually struggle with presenting numerical information in a way that is easily understood. Data visualization is crucial to minimize the cognitive load while communicating users’ finances accurately.

My Role

I was the sole designer for this passion project. My responsibilities included user research, market research, interaction design, and visual design.

Design Principles

The design principles I followed are Nielsen Norman’s heuristics:

Consistency
Recognition rather than recall
Match between system and the real world
Consistency Use the same icons and words so that our users don't get confused.
Recognition rather than recall Minimize the user's memory load by making objects, actions, and options visible.
Match between system and the real world The system should speak the users' language, with words, phrases and concepts familiar to the user, rather than system-oriented terms.

Mind Mapping

When it comes to a personal finance app, the sheer volume of numbers and charts that gets presented is nearly endless. What should Golden Years show to its users? I started with mind-mapping to ideate product concepts. Coming from a finance background, bonds and equities were the first two words I wrote down. After that, it was owning a house, enjoying the retirement life, and hanging out with grandchildren. As I kept jotting down notes, I realized that Golden Years should be more than a tool. It should be tied to people's life goals. I want to help people take care of money so that they can afford the life they want.

User Research

Duration

1 week

Research analyses showed that the target audiences were roughly divided into three categories. These personas illustrated the distinct characteristics, motivations, goals, and frustrations of their representing group.

It was clear that the top features desired in a personal finance app include ease of use, minimal input, and security. Users want to link their accounts and have a centralized platform to oversee all of their money.

Retirement Onboarding

Once new users sign up for an account, they are directed to a "Welcome" screen that walks them through a retirement assessment. User research shows that retirement is the biggest concern and Golden Years greets its users by educating them on their retirement readiness.

Three Ways to Link An Account

Based on user research, I designed three methods for users to link accounts.

  1. Select: The easiest way is to select from a suggested list.
  2. Search: Users can also search for their financial institution.
  3. Manual: For users who are concerned about privacy, they have the freedom to manually add an account. The example here demonstrates how to add a residential property and a vehicle.

Branding & Visual


Bringing Golden Years to life

Duration

1 week

Logo

The initial logo sketches included sunset on the beach and stock charts. Although the stock charts spoke directly to the function of the app, sunset on the beach painted a retirement goal that most people can relate to. After several user tests, I decided on the beach tree logo to resemble a secure and satisfying lifestyle.

Colors

Color palette went through several iterations. The goal was to create a professional and modern image. I ran a couple preference tests and users voted for blue and grey, commenting on their depth and trustworthiness to the brand. I added the lighter colors for data visualization and charts.

Retrospective

The biggest challenge for me was researching what problems our users were facing and defining what features would solve their needs. Mind mapping and asking people whom I surveyed follow-up questions have been key to setting the structure for Golden Years.

Impact

Golden Years accomplished its goal to give users a tool that’s comprehensive yet easy to use. My survey respondents especially commented on its clear navigation.

Next Steps

From a business perspective, I recommend partnering with companies such as Trulia and TrueCar to estimate client’s asset values, robo advisers such as Betterment and WealthFront for clients who are tech savvy, and institutions such as Merrill Lynch, UBS, and Wells Fargo for clients who prefer a traditional approach. Partnership with reputable brands can bring in advertising revenue and build trustworthiness for Golden Years.


From a programming perspective, I recommend using 2FA (2-factor authentication) to safely guard users’ financial and personal information.

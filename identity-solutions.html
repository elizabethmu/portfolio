Identity Solutions


Screening candidates for Uber

Summary

Uber asked my company to design a product to screen their candidates.

 

This is my story of designing Identity Solutions — a progressive web app that simplifies background verification.

Platform

Progressive web app

Duration

3.5 weeks

When

2019

Problem

Most of us have gone through a background check when we apply for a job. That usually means filling out a lengthy form that includes your social security number, education, employment, history of address, photo ID, etc. It’s a tedious process.

My Role

I was the UX designer on a cross-functional team. My responsibilities included information architecture, user flows, wireframes, and prototypes. I collaborated with a senior product manager on product features.

Design Principles

The design principles I followed are Nielsen Norman’s heuristics:

Consistency
System Feedback
Consistency Use the same icons and words so that our users don't get confused.
System Feedback Provide reassurance when users complete an action. For example, when users place an order for 20 sandwiches, the application will display 20 as a confirmation.

User Research

Duration

2 days

Users for this project is anyone applying for drive/work for Uber and getting their background checked. My discussion with the product manager concluded that our user base has very little restrictions in terms of age or demographics, because
(1) almost anyone can apply to drive for Uber
(2) anyone that has held a job has probably gone through a background check

I recruited volunteers in my office to participate in a research session that studied how they felt about background check, what steps they had to go through, what documents they had to submit, and how they wish the process would be. The biggest frustration with background check was manully filling out a lot of information. Our solution was to reduce manual input and auto capture a candidate’s personal details.

Market Research

Duration

1 day

Since Identity Solutions' goal is to simplify background checks, I looked up market players to examine how they process backgroud verification.

Whom did I research: ClearChecks, CheckMate, BackgroundChecks.com
Why I chose them: They are service providers for background checks
What I learned from them: Users want an a clean interface. Users want to minimize manual input.

User Flow

Duration

1 week

After iterating and meeting with product manager to discuss business requirements, I finalized the user flow. The core flow is to upload an ID and auto capture its information. The supporting flow is to provide the option to manually input a candidate’s information.

User Flow Evolution

My senior product manager wanted users to select ID type and region after the picture is taken, which didn’t make much sense — I walked her through a typical user journey and after putting herself in the user’s shoes she realized it wasn’t the right move. I was able to choreograph the flow in a way that was realistic and intuitive.

Wireframes


Visualizing the flows


Duration

1 week

Because most applicants use their phones to check emails, I first thought about creating a native iOS or Android app. But after looking at my company’s platform and discussion with developers, it’s better to build a progressive wb app that’s viewable on any device. I started with mobile because research showed that most candiates view their emails on a phone and prefer to run their background check without having to switch to a computer.


Prototype

Duration

1 week

After I finalized the wireframes and screen flows, I put together a couple prototypes to show Uber representatives how Identity Solutions would work.

Perfect Scenario

In this prototype, a user logs in, takes a picture of her ID, and Identity Solutions captures everything. She doesn’t need to type any information manually. This is the perfect scenario.

Not So Perfect Scenario

What happens if her ID turns out blurry, or if her information is outdated? To take these situations into consideration, I created a manual path, which means that if the her picture is blurry after three retakes, Identity Solutions will ask the candidate to manually enter her information. If there’s incorrect personal details, she also has the option to make adjustments. Even though this means manual work, the end goal is to ensure that the candidates background gets verified.

Visual Design


Bringing Identity Solutions to life

Colors

To create a coherent brand identity, I incorporated HireRight’s brand colors, which are red, black, and blue. Although our primary brand color is a red that catches users’ attention, I chose no tto use it for buttons because red typically signals caution and this could confuse users. Instead, I referenced Sephora’s color scheme and used black for call to actions.

Typography

Since Identity Solutions is a progressive web app, I wanted the font to reflect the operating system. For example, when users are on an iOS platform, the font will be SF Pro. When users are on an Android platform, the font will be Roboto.

Retrospective

Communication is key

Communication is key. There were a couple of times when I received specific design directions instead of business requirements. I initiated discussion to understand why my product manager wanted things to look a certain way and came up with iterations to show alternative methods that would be more intuitive for our users. My product manager listened to my reasons and gracefully accepted my recommendations.

Take initiatives

Although my role was more specific for this project, I still looked up market players on my own and talked to people to find out how they usually go through a background check. This helps me get a better understanding of the competitive landscape as well as the user problem. I also initiated discussions with my product manager on a daily basis to syn on the design roadmap. Taking initatives made our collaboration a pleasant experience.
